Prompt engineering is the skill of writing clear instructions for AI. Better instructions = better results.
What I learned: Context matters, be specific about format and tone, ask Claude to act as an expert, and refining prompts is part of the process.
Normal prompt: "Write about marketing." — output is generic and unfocused.
Engineered prompt: "Write a 150-word LinkedIn post for a fitness coach on why email beats social media. Conversational tone. End with a CTA." — output is specific and ready to use.
3 benefits: You save time, get higher quality answers, and unlock capabilities most people never discover.
The core takeaway is simple — prompt engineering is not a technical skill, it is a communication skill. The better you ask, the better the answer.
