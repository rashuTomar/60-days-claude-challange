# Day 23 – Customer & MVP Blueprint

## Overview

On Day 23 of the **#60DaysClaudeChallenge**, I focused on the product strategy side of startups by creating a **Customer & MVP Blueprint** for my AI-powered Food Waste Reduction Platform.

The goal was to identify the right customers, validate their problems, and define a lean MVP that solves the core problem before adding advanced features.

## Startup Concept

An AI-powered platform that predicts surplus food from restaurants and connects it with nearby NGOs, students, and low-income communities to minimize food waste through efficient redistribution.

## What I Built

* Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
* Buyer Persona
* Top 10 Customer Pain Points
* Customer Journey Map
* Buying Triggers & Customer Objections
* MVP Feature Roadmap
* MoSCoW Prioritization
* Pricing Hypothesis
* Risk Assessment
* 30-Day MVP Plan
* Founder Action Sheet

## MVP Strategy

### Build First

* Restaurant onboarding
* Surplus food listing
* NGO & recipient matching
* Volunteer assignment
* QR-based food verification
* Basic analytics dashboard

### Build Later

* Advanced AI forecasting
* Enterprise dashboards
* Carbon footprint analytics
* Government integrations
* Multi-city expansion

## Key Insights

* **Customer Clarity:** 82/100
* **Problem Severity:** 94/100
* **PMF Potential:** 84/100
* **MVP Readiness:** 72/100

### Final Verdict

**🟡 Promising but Unvalidated**

## Key Learnings

* Understand customers before building products.
* Validate assumptions through customer interviews.
* Focus on solving one core problem with a lean MVP.
* Prioritize features based on customer value, not complexity.
* Product-Market Fit is achieved through continuous iteration and user feedback.

## Next Steps

* Conduct customer interviews with restaurants and NGOs.
* Launch a pilot MVP in one metro city.
* Collect user feedback and operational data.
* Improve the AI prediction model using real-world insights.
* Iterate and scale based on validated demand.
